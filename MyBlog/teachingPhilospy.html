My Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy is based on the belief that technology education should empower students with practical skills, creativity, and problem-solving abilities. With a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science and a Master's degree in Data Science and Machine Learning, I aim to provide students with both strong theoretical knowledge and practical coding experience.

As an IT teacher, my goal is to make coding engaging and accessible for all students. I believe programming is best learned through practice, projects, and real-world applications. Therefore, I encourage students to participate in coding exercises, collaborative projects, and hands-on activities that help them understand how technology solves real problems.

I also believe that every student learns in different ways. My classroom environment focuses on support, curiosity, and innovation. I guide students step-by-step through programming concepts while encouraging them to explore their own ideas and build confidence in their technical abilities.

In addition, I introduce students to modern technologies such as artificial intelligence, machine learning, and data analysis so they can understand how technology is shaping the future.

Ultimately, my mission as an IT educator is to inspire students to become creative thinkers, lifelong learners, and responsible technology users who can contribute positively to the digital world.
